Introduction

Serbia & Montenegro participated in the first six WE World Cups, with their best performance being two consecutive quarterfinal appearances in World Cup II and World Cup III. As the name would suggest, the team was made up of players from the countries of Serbia and Montenegro. After World Cup VI, in which the team failed to qualify, Montenegro declared independence. Serbia fielded a team for World Cup VII, though Montenegro did not. However, by World Cup VIII Montenegro had assembled their own squad and both entered the tournament, though neither qualified to the World Cup. The two nations have fielded separate teams ever since.

Serbia & Montenegro never got a chance to play in a Euro Cup. The first edition, Euro Cup I, was held after World Cup VIII, so both Serbia and Montenegro competed independently.

They were considered to be one of the best teams in Europe. At the time of the breakup, according to rankings, the top ten teams in UEFA were: 1. Norway, 2. France, 3. Netherlands, 4. Portugal, 5. Georgia, 6. Turkey, 7. Romania, 8. Serbia & Montenegro, 9. England, 10. Greece. Their final competitive record was 19-24-10 (W-D-L).

  • First International: Serbia & Montenegro 1 - 2 France (World Cup I Qualifying)
  • Last International: Slovenia 1 - 0 Serbia & Montenegro (World Cup VI Qualifying)
  • Biggest Win: Serbia & Montenegro 3 - 0 Chile (World Cup II Group Stage)
  • Biggest Defeat: Serbia & Montenegro 0 - 3 Norway (World Cup IV Qualifying)
  • World Cup Appearances: 4
  • Best World Cup Result: Quarterfinals (World Cup II, World Cup III)
