Introduction

This was the eleventh World Cup and for the first time ever, there were no new teams participating. All 113 teams who had participated in World Cup X entered the qualification stage again.

Since there was no change to the number of teams, there was also no change to the format. The previous tournament's format had worked so well that it was kept completely in tact for this tournament. It was possible for each continent to send: 10-20 nations from Europe, 5-13 from Africa, 2-10 from Asia, 3-8 from South America, and 2-8 from North America. In the final 32 were 13 nations from Europe, 6 from Africa, 6 from South America, 4 from Asia, and 3 from North America.

The Draw

Since there was no change to the format, for the first time since World Cup II, seeding was based solely on the results of the previous tournament only. All group winners from World Cup X were given the highest seed in this tournament, and so on down to the last placed team in each group being placed in the bottom pot.

The Format

UEFA

UEFA's format was straightforward, 10 groups of 4 - group winners would qualify directly to the World Cup, runners up would advance to the playoffs, third and fourth place teams would be eliminated.

CAF

CAF had 5 groups of 5, where the group winners would all qualify to the World Cup. All 5 runners up would advance to the playoffs, along with the "best third place" team. The other 4 third place teams would be paired up to play an aggregate, where the 2 winners would advance to the playoffs, and the losers would be eliminated. In total, 5 teams would qualify directly and 8 would advance to the playoffs.


CONMEBOL

CONMEBOL would use 3 groups of 4 and have the group winners all qualify directly. All second place teams would advance to the playoffs and the "best third place" team would also advance, along with the winner of an aggregate between the two other third place teams. In total, 3 teams would qualify directly and 5 would advance to the playoffs.


CONCACAF

CONCACAF had 4 groups of 4, but only 2 automatic spots. They also had a half spot to be split with AFC, for a potential third automatic spot. As such, a "second round" would be played between all four group winners. The top two teams would qualify directly to the World Cup, the third place team would go to the AFC playoff, and the fourth place team would join all of the runners up in the playoffs. In total CONCACAF had 2 direct spots, a half spot against AFC, and 5 playoff spots.

AFC

AFC had a similar situation as CONCACAF, in which they had more groups than direct spots. They had 5 groups of 4 with 2 direct spots and 1 half spot to split with CONCACAF. This also lent itself to a "second round" where all five group winners would play a round robin to determine placement. The top two would qualify directly to the World Cup, the third place team would go on to play in the CONCACAF playoff, and the bottom two would join the group runners up in the playoffs. AFC had a total of 2 automatic spots, one half spot against CONCACAF, and 7 playoff spots.


AFC vs CONCACAF Playoff

AFC and CONCACAF each had 2.5 automatic spots for the World Cup, so their third place teams would play off to determine which team got that full spot. The loser would go into the playoffs with the other teams to fight for one of the remaining 9 spots.


Playoffs

36 teams would play aggregates to narrow the field down to 18, and then those remaining teams would play aggregates to determine the 9 qualifiers.
