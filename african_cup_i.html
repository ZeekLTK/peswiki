Introduction

This was the first African Cup to be contested and it was played shortly after World Cup VIII. There were a total of 20 teams who took part in the competition, as all sixteen teams that had participated in World Cup VIII qualifying entered and the tournament also saw the re-emergence of Burkina Faso, Cape Verde Islands, and Mozambique who had not fielded teams since World Cup VI. Gabon made their WEFA debut in this tournament as well.

Rather than a traditional sixteen team tournament, CAF decided to use a unique ten team format for the "finals" like several other continents used for their inaugural competitions.

Burkina Faso broke several records during their home match against Tunisia in qualifying. Their 8-0 victory currently stands as the largest margin of victory and eight goals is the most any team has ever scored in a WEFA competitive match.
  • Teams Entered: 20
  • Champion: Morocco (1st)
  • Top Scorer: Manucho (5)


Qualification

The twenty teams were broken up into five groups of four, to be played in a double round robin (home and away) format. The top 2 teams in each group would qualify to the finals.

Seeding

The draw for the groups was seeded.

Seeding was based on each nation's performance in the previous eight World Cups. Pot E contained all of the teams who had never qualified to a World Cup. Pot D contained all teams who had qualified only once or twice. Pot C contained all teams who had qualified 3 times. Pot A contained most teams who had qualified 4 or more times. Pot B was a special pot containing Tunisia and Cameroon who were tied in appearances (4) and "best finish" (round of sixteen) - one was to be drawn into a group with Pot A teams, the other with Pot C teams. (Cameroon were drawn into Pot A)

Pot A: Cote d'Ivoire, Nigeria, Morocco, South Africa
Pot B: Tunisia, Cameroon,
Pot C: Mali, Senegal, Togo, Egypt
Pot D: Mozambique, Angola, Ghana, Congo DR, Burkina Faso
Pot E: Algeria, Cape Verde Islands, Congo, Gabon, Guinea

African Cup

Despite being held at a neutral venue, the home/away designation still mattered, so the tournament used groups of 5 rather than groups of 4 to ensure fairness. Each team was designated as the "home team" for two games, and "away team" for two games. Group stage results reflected the designations in the quarterfinals and semifinals.

In the group stage, "home" teams won 11 games, drew 7, and only lost 2. All "home" teams advanced in both quarterfinal and semifinal rounds (3 wins, 1 draw with PKs).
