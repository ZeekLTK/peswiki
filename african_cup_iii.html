Introduction

This was the third African Cup to be contested and it was played shortly after Confederations Cup II. There were a total of 25 teams who took part in the competition, as all 22 teams returned from the second African Cup while Benin, Reunion, and Sierra Leone entered for the first time.

Qualification

The defending champions, Nigeria, qualified directly. The remaining 24 teams were divided into 6 groups of 4, where the top 2 teams would qualify and the 3rd place teams would play-off for the final 3 spots.

Seeding

The draw for the groups was seeded.

Seeding was based on each nation's performance in the previous World Cups and African Cups. Results were averaged to try to level the field for newer teams compared to teams that had been competing since the very first World Cup.

Pot A: Cote d'Ivoire, Mozambique, Morocco, Mali, Cape Verde Islands, Equatorial Guinea
Pot B: Senegal, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Angola, South Africa, Togo
Pot C: Congo, Tunisia, Ghana, Congo DR, Egypt, Algeria
Pot D: Gabon, Zambia, Guinea, Sierra Leone, Benin, Reunion
