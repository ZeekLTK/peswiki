Introduction

This was the first Confederations Cup to be contested, and was held after World Cup IX. All 7 former World Cup champions were invited along with all 5 continental champions, for 12 total teams. The format was the same as Gold Cup I with 3 groups of 4, and then a staggered-entry knockout round.

  • Teams Entered: 12
  • Champion: Spain (1st)
  • Top Scorer: D. Milito (5)

Qualification

-France qualified as World Cup I champion.
-Georgia qualified as World Cup II champion.
-Cote d'Ivoire qualified as World Cup III champion.
-Norway qualified as World Cup V champion.
-England qualified as World Cup VII champion.
-Argentina qualified as World Cup VIII champion.
-Italy qualified as World Cup IX champion.

-Bahrain qualified as Asian Cup I champion.
-Brazil qualified as Copa America I champion.
-Jamaica qualified as Gold Cup I champion.
-Morocco qualified as African Cup I champion.
-Spain qualified as Euro Cup I champion.

Draw

Pot A: France, Georgia, Norway (early WC champions)
Pot B: England, Italy, Spain (modern WC champions + Euro champion)
Pot C: Cote d'Ivoire, Morocco (Africa)
Pot D: Argentina, Brazil (South America)
Pot E: Jamaica (North America)
Pot F: Bahrain (Asia)
