Introduction

Norway is a European nation who competes in UEFA for international competitions. They are one of the most successful teams in the world, having won the World Cup twice. Only Georgia has won as many titles. Norway also earned the distinction of being the first (and so far, only) team to repeat as champions, having won back-to-back titles in World Cup V and World Cup VI. During World Cup V Norway won every single game in all rounds of the tournament, including qualifying, the group stage, and the knockout round, finishing with a 15-0-0 record.

  • First International: Norway 3 - 2 Russia (World Cup I Qualifying)
  • Biggest Win: Ireland 0 - 7 Norway (World Cup IV Qualifying), Romania 0 - 7 Norway (World Cup IV Qualifying)
  • Biggest Defeat: Norway 1 - 7 Brazil (Confederations Cup I Third Place)
  • Euro Cup Appearances: 2
  • Best Euro Cup Result: Champions (Euro Cup II)
  • World Cup Appearances: 9
  • Best World Cup Result: Champions (World Cup V, World Cup VI)


Early World Cups

Despite being a completely random draw, Norway were drawn into somewhat of a Northern European group for qualifying to the first World Cup, pairing up with Russia, Sweden, and Latvia. Norway started with an impressive 3-2 win over Russia and an even more impressive 2-0 victory over neighbors Sweden. With a spot in the World Cup already secure, the team sent out backups to face Latvia and managed a 1-1 draw to top the group.

In the World Cup, Norway were drawn into a very tough group with Brazil, Czech Republic, and Morocco. The Norwegians got off a great start by beating Morocco 5-2 in the opening game, their largest ever margin of victory in a competitive match at the time, and then managed to tie the group favorites, Brazil, 0-0 in the next match. Everyone ended up beating Morocco, and Brazil had won against the Czechs on the opening day, so it came down to Norway vs Czech Republic for a spot in the next round. The Czechs proved to be too tough and won 1-0 to eliminate Norway from the World Cup.

Norway dusted themselves off and attempted to qualify for the next World Cup. This time they were drawn into a much more difficult qualifying group, with England, Spain, and Croatia. A 2-0 win over Croatia to start the campaign increased the spirits of the team, but a setback in the form of a 1-3 defeat to England put the Norwegians in a must win situation against Spain in the final match. Unlike the previous World Cup, Norway overcame their stronger opponents and were rewarded with a 1-0 victory to take 2nd place in the group with 6 points, behind England's 7. This was enough to earn one of the "best second place" qualifying spots to the World Cup, so Norway did not have to go through the play-offs.

The draw for this World Cup treated Norway much nicer as they ended up in a group with Jamaica, Ukraine, and Morocco again. Again, Norway played Morocco in the opening game. The Africans were determined to get revenge for the previous World Cup, but were unable to. The scoreline wasn't as embarassing this time, just 2-0, but it was enough for Norway. Norway would go on to defeat Jamaica 3-1 in the next match and then tied 1-1 with the Ukraine to get their 7th point and top the group ahead of Morocco and Ukraine who each had 4, and Jamaica who had just 1 point.

Norway lucked out in the knockout round. They were projected to face Brazil, but Tunisia managed to shock the world and advance instead thanks to a 1-0 win over Brazil on the last matchday. Tunisia proved to be tougher than expected (at least compared to Morocco), but Norway still managed to move on with a 1-0 result. In the quarterfinals the team came up against Serbia & Montenegro who had taken down Denmark in the previous round. The match turned out to be a great one, and it couldn't be settled in 90 minutes, with the score level at 1-1 heading into overtime. Norway scored the winner before the match could go to penalties and moved on to the semifinals where they met the defending champions, France.

Norway put up a great fight against France, but they just could not break through the French defense. The team did very well to hold France in check for as long as possible, but finally one of the French strikers broke through and beat the keeper for the eventual winning goal as France moved on to the final. Norway had to settle for the third place match against Costa Rica. Uninhibited, Norway went after the Central Americans and managed to get the most goals they've scored since that 5-2 win over Morocco in the last World Cup. The defense conceded a few themselves, but when the dust had settled it was 4-2 to Norway and they had earned the bronze.


World Series

Norway also participated in the World Series. The tournament took a lot of flak for its random draw which saw Norway, third place from the last World Cup, paired up against France, who had taken second. This was a matchup most people were hoping for in the quarterfinals or later, but it was going to happen right away in the first round instead.

Norway saw it as a chance to redeem themselves from the World Cup, and started strong when John Carew scored in the opening minute of the first game in Oslo. The goal wasn't enough though, and France eventually struck back to equalize and then take the lead, eventually winning the match 2-1. Norway didn't give up though, and leveled the series at 1-1 by tieing game 2 1-1 to send it into overtime where Flo scored his second goal of the match to win the game for Norway.

Back in France, home field advantage proved to be too much for Norway to overcome. In Game 3, the French bombarded Norway, putting 5 goals past them in the 5-2 route. Game 4 was less harsh, but produced a similar result, with France winning 3-2 to take a 3-1 lead in the series. Norway refused to be eliminated at home and ended up grinding out a hard fought 2-1 victory to force the series back to France. In Paris, Norway again failed to overcome the home side in a match that resembled the World Cup semifinal where France won 1-0 to take the series 4 games to 2 and eliminated Norway in the opening round.

Generally Norway plays with mostly defenders and strikers, although they use traditional formations, usually alternating between two forms of the 3-4-3 and occassionally a 3-6-1. Critics (and some fans) of the system refer to it as the 5-0-5 since there really are no true midfielders in the starting lineup.

Lately the team has begun to try to include midfielders like Andresen and Rushfeldt into the lineup, but traditionally Norway attempts to ignore the midfield and focuses on their primary goal of ensuring the opposition struggles on offense. Due to this school of thought that "defense always comes before offense", Norway have come up with this type of formation to ensure they are secure on defense but also not completely inept on offense since they are generally not a good offensive side.
