Early World Cups

Israel was not an original team in WE8 and thus did not participate in the early World Cups. They were eventually created following World Cup V and participated for the first time in World Cup VI qualifying.

World Cup Qualifying in UEFA

Isreal was drawn into UEFA Group F for their first ever qualifying campaign, in World Cup VI. Their very first match was against Switzerland, in which they managed a 1-1 draw as Golan scored their first ever goal. Other teams in the group included France, Sweden, Romania, and Poland. Despite the positive start, Israel would struggle throughout qualifying, failing to win any of their first seven games. Finally, in their eighth match, Israel shocked France with a goal by Benayoun to pick up a 1-0 victory over the former champions. It would be Israel's only win of the campaign, as they drew and then lost their last two matches to finish the group with 8 points from 1 win, 5 draws, and 4 losses. Israel were last place in the group, although they were only three points behind both Sweden and Switzerland. Their sole victory ended up impacting the final standings too, as it caused France to finish two points behind Romania. Both teams still qualified to the World Cup though.

For World Cup VII, Israel were drawn into UEFA Group C against Russia, Denmark, and Wales. Israel failed to win a single game in this group, only managing two draws against Denmark while losing both games against each other opponent. Obviously Israel again finished in last place with just two points and failed to qualify yet again.

In World Cup VIII, Israel were again drawn into UEFA Group F, although this time against Turkey, Ukraine, and Finland. Israel started strong, a 2-2 draw against Ukraine followed by a shocking 2-1 win over Turkey propelled the Israelis into an early tie for first. Everything fell apart during the next two matches, both against Finland, as Israel lost both to fall out of the top spot. Another draw with Ukraine sealed both nations' fate as they had each needed a win to remain in the hunt for the qualification spot. Both ended up losing their last match anyways, and Israel narrowly avoided last place by virtue of goal difference since they were tied with Ukraine on 5 points apiece.

Move to Asia

World Cup VIII was originally slated to begin with 86 nations, but at the very last minute New Zealand submitted a bid to participate. This happened after the qualifying groups had already been drawn, but WEFA decided to let them participate, and since most of the AFC groups included first time teams, New Zealand was just drawn into a random group and it was made a group of five while the rest of Asia used groups of four.

Uneven qualifying groups are generally frowned upon by WEFA though, so following World Cup VIII they declared that Asia needed to balance itself. That meant they would need to add a member to get to an even 18, which would allow them to use three groups of six in the next campaign. Indonesia submitted a bid to become the 18th member, but had only 12 players included in their bid, including a few that had already been capped by Netherlands. This may have been accepted in the "Winning Eleven era", but not for PES10. Instead, with no other Asian nations having a serious bid, the AFC invited Israel to join. Since Israel is located in the Middle East, along with many other AFC nations, the move was approved by WEFA.

Indonesia eventually put together a full squad and joined as the 19th member. However, rather than have Israel move back to UEFA, Georgia joined the confederation to increase membership to 20 teams.
