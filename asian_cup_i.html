Introduction

This was the first Asian Cup to be contested and it was played shortly after World Cup VIII. There were a total of 18 teams who took part in the competition; all seventeen teams who participated in World Cup VIII qualifying plus Israel, who had moved to AFC from UEFA for the tournament to give both AFC and UEFA an even number of teams to use in their tournaments (18 and 40 respectively).

Rather than a traditional sixteen team tournament, AFC decided to use a unique ten team format for the "finals", so that it could eliminate eight teams in qualifying rather than just two. The top two in each group would advance to the knockout round, which would start at the semifinal stage.
  • Teams Entered: 18
  • Champion: Bahrain (1st)
  • Top Scorer: A. Hubail (6)

Qualification

The eighteen teams were broken up into four uneven groups. Two groups would have five teams and two groups would have four teams. Qualification was straight forward, the top 3 teams in the groups of five would qualify while only the top 2 from the groups of four would qualify.

Seeding

The draw for the groups was seeded. The draw was also non-conventional in that it only used three "tiers" rather than 5 as a five team group draw would require. The rational behind this was that there were many "new" teams and that there was little to distinguish between them to determine who deserved a higher seed.

Seeding was based on each nation's performance in the previous World Cups. The four teams who had qualified the most were placed into Pot A. There were only three other nations who had qualified, so they were placed in Pot B along with Iraq and Qatar - teams who had reached the playoffs of World Cup VIII but had failed to qualify. All other teams who had never qualified were placed into Pot C.

Pot A: Australia, South Korea, Japan, Iran
Pot B: Saudi Arabia, China, Uzebekistan, Iraq, Qatar
Pot C: Bahrain, Israel, Kuwait, New Zealand, North Korea, Oman, Syria, Thailand, United Arab Emirates
