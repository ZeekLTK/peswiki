Introduction

This was the first Euro Cup to be contested and it was played after World Cup VIII. There were a total of 40 teams who took part in the competition; all of the UEFA sides who had participated in World Cup VIII qualifying (execpt Israel, who played in Asian Cup I instead) and debutants Albania, Belarus, Estonia, and Lithuania. Also, Latvia returned for their first competition since World Cup VI.
  • Teams Entered: 40
  • Champion: Spain (1st)
  • Top Scorer: Jankauskas (5)

Euro Cup

Seeding

Seeding was based on qualification performance. All group winners were put into pots A and B, all runners up into pots C and D, based on points earned and then goal difference.

Pot A: Netherlands, France, Italy, Turkey
Pot B: Norway, England, Georgia, Portugal
Pot C: Spain, Bulgaria, Germany, Romania
Pot D: Lithuania, Ireland, Sweden, Wales
